## Projects

### 1. Heart Disease Prediction Model
`Python` `Machine Learning` `Scikit-learn`

Built and evaluated multiple classification models to predict heart
disease risk from clinical data. Models explored include Decision Tree,
XGBoost, AdaBoost, Artificial Neural Networks, and Logistic Regression.
Evaluation focused on precision, recall, and accuracy trade-offs across
models to identify the most clinically appropriate approach.

**Business relevance:** Risk stratification and predictive modelling
for health outcomes — directly applicable to healthcare analytics
and AI-augmented clinical decision support.

---

### 2. Damaged Smartphone Image Classification
`Python` `Deep Learning` `Transfer Learning` `VGG16`

Designed an image classification pipeline using K-Means Clustering
and Transfer Learning with a pre-trained VGG16 model from Keras. The
approach leveraged an existing validated model to reduce training time
and computational cost while maintaining strong classification accuracy.

**Business relevance:** Computer vision and transfer learning for
quality assurance and asset management, with applications in
insurance, logistics, and retail.

---

### 3. Best-Selling Smartphone Prediction (Amazon Data)
`Python` `Logistic Regression` `Random Forest`

Applied supervised learning models to Amazon product data to identify
the key factors driving demand for consumer electronics. Compared
Logistic Regression and Random Forest approaches for feature importance
and predictive accuracy.

**Business relevance:** Demand forecasting and product analytics for
ecommerce and retail clients, supporting inventory and marketing
strategy decisions.
